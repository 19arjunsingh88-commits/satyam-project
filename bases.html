🧼 Bases Library

Important bases with chemical formulas

⚡ Strong Bases
Sodium Hydroxide → NaOH
Potassium Hydroxide → KOH
Calcium Hydroxide → Ca(OH)₂
Barium Hydroxide → Ba(OH)₂
Lithium Hydroxide → LiOH
🧪 Weak Bases
Ammonia → NH₃ (most common weak base)
Ammonium Hydroxide → NH₄OH
Magnesium Hydroxide → Mg(OH)₂
Aluminium Hydroxide → Al(OH)₃
🔬 Special Bases
Zinc Hydroxide → Zn(OH)₂
Iron(II) Hydroxide → Fe(OH)₂
Iron(III) Hydroxide → Fe(OH)₃
Copper Hydroxide → Cu(OH)₂
Sodium Carbonate → Na₂CO₃ (basic salt)
💡 Quick Fact
Bases taste bitter and feel slippery in water.
They turn red litmus paper blue.
