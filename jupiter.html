🟠 Jupiter

The Giant King of the Solar System

📖 About Jupiter
Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System and is often called the “King of Planets” because of its massive size, powerful gravity, and dominant presence. It is the fifth planet from the Sun and is so huge that more than 1300 Earths could fit inside it.

Jupiter is a gas giant composed mainly of hydrogen and helium. Unlike Earth, it does not have a solid surface. Instead, it consists of thick clouds and powerful storms that move at extremely high speeds.

One of its most famous features is the Great Red Spot, a gigantic storm that has been active for hundreds of years and is larger than Earth itself.

Jupiter also has an extremely strong magnetic field and more than 90 moons, including Ganymede — the largest moon in the Solar System.

This planet also acts as a cosmic shield, attracting many comets and asteroids, helping protect inner planets like Earth from impacts.

In simple words, Jupiter is not just a planet — it is a giant world that helps maintain balance in the Solar System.
