🌍 Earth

The Only Known Living Planet

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the most unique planet in the entire Solar System because it is the only known world that supports life. It is often called the “Blue Planet” due to the large amount of water covering its surface, which appears blue from space.

About 71% of Earth’s surface is covered with oceans, seas, and water bodies, while the remaining part consists of continents, mountains, deserts, and forests. This balance of land and water makes Earth perfectly suitable for life to exist.

Earth has a protective layer called the atmosphere, which contains gases like oxygen and nitrogen. This atmosphere protects all living beings from harmful solar radiation and helps maintain a stable climate.

The planet also has a natural satellite called the Moon. The Moon plays an important role in controlling ocean tides and also makes the night sky beautiful.

Earth completes one full rotation in 24 hours, which creates day and night, and takes 365 days to complete one revolution around the Sun, forming a year.

Life on Earth exists in many forms — humans, animals, birds, insects, plants, and microorganisms. Every organism plays an important role in maintaining the balance of nature.

In simple words, Earth is not just a planet, it is our home, and protecting it is the responsibility of every living being.
