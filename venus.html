🌕 Venus

The Hottest Planet in the Solar System

📖 About Venus
Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth’s twin because of its similar size and structure.

But despite similarities, Venus is extremely different — it is the hottest planet in the Solar System due to its thick atmosphere that traps heat like a greenhouse.

Its atmosphere is mostly carbon dioxide with thick clouds of sulfuric acid, making it a very hostile environment.

Venus rotates very slowly, and one day on Venus is longer than its entire year.
🌌 10 Interesting Facts about Venus

Venus Facts

1. Venus is the second planet from the Sun.

2. It is the hottest planet in the Solar System.

3. Venus is similar in size to Earth.

4. It has a thick carbon dioxide atmosphere.

5. Clouds are made of sulfuric acid.

6. Surface temperature is about 465°C.

7. Venus rotates very slowly.

8. One day is longer than its year.

9. It is known as Morning Star / Evening Star.

10. Venus has no moons or rings.
